Chi2 test

After numeric features discretization, every feature has discrete value, so does Class, the label. Therefore, we apply Chi2 test to filter features with low correlation to Class.

Chi2 test is used for testing correlation between discrete variables. It is achieved through R x C contingency table. Take Chi2 test of feature raisedHands for example. In addition to selecting features, we use p-value of chi2 test to measure feature importance. Feature that is more important has a lower p-value. Since some features has an extremely low p-value, we use logarithm of p-value instead of raw p-value.

The following bar chart shows features and their corresponding logarithm of p-value.

It's obvious that StudentAbsenceDays has the lowest p-value, which means it's the most important feature. This is reasonable because the number of absence days directly influence how much students learn.

Meanwhile, Discussion, raisedHands, VisitedResources and AnnouncementsView these four numeric features also have a low p-value, indicating they are also important. This is the case in our life, since value of the above four features reveal students' learning enthusiasm.

Most of Nationality and Topic have a high p-value and thus are unimportant features. Only a few Nationality, PlaceofBirth and Topic have a relaively low p-value. This indicates there isn't obvious change of students' performance over most of nations and topics, but a few topics and nations influence students's grade a little more greatly.

What's more, gender also has a great impact on students' Class. This is true in real life.

Finally, ParentAnsweringSurvey, Relation and ParentschoolSatisfaction are important features, with p-value around . This shows that parents' paticipation in students' education plays a great role in students' academic performance.
